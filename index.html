Overlake Spring Makeathon

An event for WAWG cadets, focused on modeling an aircraft or spacecraft using NASA's software in an innovate, virtual learning environment.

Register

📣 Imagine, design, create.

Have you ever dreamed of creatingn your aircraft? WA-050 is bringing a unique opportunity to learn how to work with OpenVSP, flight software used by NASA to create air and spacecraft models. Within 40 hours, students will go from complete beginners to having created their very own aircraft - being able to imagine a concept and model it virtually.

📝 Skills and Lessons


Cadets will be led through instruction and lessons to ensure they are familiar with the software and concepts before they are given project prompts and begin working on their projects. Through allowing cadets to learn a new concept quckly in a supportive environment, we ensure that they will walk away with:

  • Principles of aircraft design
  • OpenVSP, a NASA software
  • Preparation for hackathons and makeathons
  • Virtual teamwork & collaboration skills



🏆 Prizes & Awards

Cadets will submit their aircraft designs, which will be judged by a panel and prizes will be presented in several categories. Prizes will be announced soon!

❔ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need aircraft design or modeling experience to participate?

Not at all! During the first few hours of the Makeathon, you'll attend several workshops that will lay down the basics. This Makeathon is aimed at complete beginners, so you'll be provided with lots of resoures, as well as mentors to help you with any questions or issues you run into.

I registered for the event. What do I do next?

Make sure to view the Student Guide, which will have information about pre-event setup. You'll get an email going over more information as the event nears. If you are unable to complete the steps on the Student Guide, please email C/1st Lt Dubiel ASAP.

Will we be working in groups?

Cadets will be working in groups of 2 or 3. They will get the opportunity to choose a group, and those who have not partnered up will take a survey and be grouped together by the event staff.

How long is the event?

Event scheduling is still TBD. Current estimates are from Saturday morning to Sunday night in early or mid April.

Who should I contact regarding questions about registration or the event?

Send any questions to C/1st Lt Dubiel (karolina.dubiel@wawg.cap.gov) or Maj Clement Ng (clement.ng@wawg.cap.gov).
